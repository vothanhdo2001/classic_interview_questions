- No plan - just talking and hoping for the best
- Missing the chance to stand out

## SLIDE 3 - The 4 Classic Questions (Roadmap)
**Headline:** 6 Questions. 1 Framework. Endless Opportunities.

**Content (numbered list):**
1. Tell me about yourself
2. Why do you want this job?
3. What is your greatest strength?
4. What is your greatest weakness?
5. Tell me about a mistake you made
6. Do you have any questions for us?

**Visual idea:** Numbered roadmap or stepping stones graphic

---

## SLIDE 4 - Q1: Tell Me About Yourself
**Headline:** Q1: "Tell me about yourself"

**Formula box:**
```
Background  →  Key Skill  →  Why This Role
```

**Key point:** 30-second structured answer - not your life story

**Example tip:**
> "Keep it crisp: who you are, what you bring, and why you're here."

**Useful phrases:**
- *"I'm particularly proud of..."* - to introduce a real achievement
- *"A good example that comes to mind..."* - to bring in a story
- *"Timekeeping is important to me - I always plan ahead to meet deadlines."*

**Visual idea:** Stopwatch icon / speech bubble with 3-part formula

---

## SLIDE 5 - Q2: Why Do You Want This Job?
**Headline:** Q2: "Why do you want this job?"

**Key message:** Prove you've done your homework

**3 bullets:**
- Find out about the company's goals and values
- Connect what they care about to your own story
- Show real interest, not just "I need a job"

**Pro tip box:**
> "People who truly know the company always stand out."

**If stuck for words:**
> "I really, really want this job and I am willing to work very hard if I get it."

**Visual idea:** Magnifying glass over a company logo / research icon

---

## SLIDE 6 - Q3: Greatest Strength
**Headline:** Q3: "What is your greatest strength?"

**Formula (use connectors to structure your story):**
```
Strength  →  Firstly (what happened)  →  Secondly (what you did)  →  Above all (the result)
```

**Example:**
> "My strength is staying calm when things get difficult. Firstly, when my team's deadline moved up by one week, I didn't panic. Secondly, I made a new plan and reorganized the tasks. And above all, we finished on time."

**Key word:** **Be specific - one strength, one story**

**Visual idea:** Trophy icon / before-after chart

---

## SLIDE 7 - Q4: Greatest Weakness
**Headline:** Q4: "What is your greatest weakness?"

**What NOT to do:**
- ~~"I work too hard"~~ - interviewers always see through this

**What TO do:**
- Choose a real weakness you are working to fix
- Show what you are doing about it right now

**Example:**
> "I used to avoid hard conversations. So I started asking for feedback after every project. That helped me get better."

**Key message:** Honest + Working to improve = Trust

**Visual idea:** Arrow going upward / growth chart

---

## SLIDE 8 - Q5: A Mistake You Made
**Headline:** Q5: "Tell me about a mistake you made"

**Formula:**
```
Mistake  →  Lesson Learned  →  How I Fixed It  →  Positive Outcome
```

**Key message:** Employers want someone who takes responsibility - not someone who is perfect

**Quote:**
> "Saying 'I made a mistake and here is what I did about it' shows more than saying 'I never make mistakes.'"

**Visual idea:** Broken piece being repaired / lesson icon

---

## SLIDE 9 - Q6: Do You Have Questions?
**Headline:** Q6: "Do you have any questions for us?"

**Never say:** "No, I think we covered everything."

**What to do instead:**
- Say in one sentence why you are a good fit
- Ask one question about the future

**Power question:**
> "Is there a problem your team is facing right now that you think I could help with?"

**Visual idea:** Dialogue bubbles / handshake icon
